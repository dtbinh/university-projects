Module Beskrywing

Verskeie toepassings van getalleteorie, modulere rekenkunde en abstrakte algebra in die wetenskaplike sfere van kriptologie (die beveiliging van sensitiewe inlingting/data) en kodeerteorie (die integriteit van inligting/data) vorm die oorkopelende tema van hierdie module. Beide kriptologie en kodeerteorie is sentrale studievelde in die huidige, moderne era van informasietegnologie, waar groot hoeveelhede inligting gedurig elektronies via telefoonlyne en die internet versend word. Studente wat hierdie module gevolg het, is in 'n ideale posisie om nuwe sisteme vir beide die geheimhouding en beveiliging van die integriteit van elektronies versende inligting te ontwerp en bestaande sisteme te analiseer. Kriptologiese onderwerpe wat in die module behandel word, sluit in bekende bloksyfer stelsels (soos die Caesar sisteem, die Vigenere sisteem, die Hill sisteem, DES en IDEA), stoomsyfer stelsels (en spesifiek die gebruik van lineere terugvoer skuifregisters om bisstrome te genereer) sowel as publieke sleutel sisteme (soos die ElGamal en RSA sisteme). Onderwerpe in kodeerteorie wat in die module behandel word, sluit in Hamming afstande en foutkorrigerende kodes. Die wiskundige begrippe onderliggend tot hierdie twee toepassingsvelde word in diepte behandel en deur middel van reeds geimplementeerde rekenaarpakkette geillustreer. Alhoewel rekenaargeletterdheid vir hierdie module essensieel is, word rekenaar programmeringsvaardighede nie vereis nie.

Module Description

Various applications of number theory, modular arithmetic and abstract algebra within the scientific fields of cryptology (the protection of sensitive information/data) and coding theory (the integrity of information/data) form the main theme of this module. Both cryptology and coding theory are central fields of interest in the modern age of information technology where large quantities of information are continually being transmitted electronically via telephone lines and the internet. Students who have taken this course are in an ideal position to develop new systems and analyse existing systems for the protection of both the integrity and privacy of data being transmitted electronically. Cryptographical topics include well-known block ciphers (such as the Caesar system, the Vigenère system, the Hill system, DES and IDEA), stream ciphers (and specifically the use of linear feedback registers to create key streams) as well as public key systems (such as the ElGamal and RSA systems). Topics in coding theory include Hamming distances and error correcting codes. The mathematical theory behind these application fields is treated in some detail and illustrated by using ready implemented computer packages throughout the course. Although computer literacy is essential, computer programming skills are not reuired.


Module Uitkomste


'n Student wat hierdie module suksesvol voltooi het, kan: / A student who has successfully completed this module, can:
  1. die vertroulikheid van sensitiewe inligting met behulp van elementere tegnieke uit klassieke en moderne kriptologie verseker, deur die implementasie van 'n wye seleksie sisteme uit die drie bree klasse van bloksyfer stelsels, stroomsyfer stelsels en publieke sleutel stelsels.
  2. ingeligte uitsprake lewer aangaande die praktiese doeltreffendheid en voorwaardelike sekuriteit van 'n groot aantal kriptosisteme uit die bogenoemde drie bree klasse stelsels.
  3. die wiskundige meganismes agter 'n verskeidenheid kriptosisteme wat hedendaags wereldwyd in gebruik is, verklaar en ontleed.
  4. sinvolle en effektiewe kripto-analitiese aanslae op 'n groot aantal kriptosisteme loods deur middel van 'n verskeidenheid wiskundige tegnieke.
  5. die integriteit van inligting wat elektronies deur kanale met geruis versend word, analiseer en waar moontlik foutkorreksie-tegnieke toepas.

Module Outcomes


A student who has successfully completed this module, can:
  1. ensure the confidentiality of sensitive information via elementary methods from classical as well as modern cryptology, by implementing a wide variety of systems from the three broad classes of block ciphers, stream ciphers and public key ciphers.
  2. draw knowledgeable conclusions about the practical efficiency and conditional security of a large number of cryptographic ciphers from the above mentioned three broad classes of systems.
  3. understand and analyse the mathematical mechanisms behind a wide variety of cryptographic cihpers currently in use thoughout the world.
  4. launch meaningful and efficient crypto-analytic attacks against a wide variety of cryptographic cicphers, using mathematial techniques.
  5. analyse the integrity of and (where necessary) apply error-correcting techniques to information transmitted over a noisy communication channel.

Module Assessment


This module is examined by means of continued evaluation. This means that there will be no examinations or supplementary examinations for this module. The final mark will be calculated as follows:

Evaluation Session Percentage
Semester Test 1 (April) 20%
Semester Test 2 (June) 25%
Assignment 1 (Cryptology) 7.5%
Assignment 2 (Coding Theory) 7.5%
Tutorials (10 Opportunities - each 4% of the final mark) 40%

The largest proportion of assessment opportunities that a student may miss, without being awarded an incomplete for the module, is 12% (in other words the equivalent of 3 tutorials).
  • If a student misses a tutorial as a result of illness, the accumulated average of his/her remaining tutorial marks will be awarded for that tutorial, if the student is able to produce a valid medical certificate (provided that the bound of 12% mentioned above is not exceeded). However, if a student misses a tutorial because of a reason other than illness, an absent (zero) will be awarded for that tutorial (provided that the bound of 12% mentioned above is not exceeded - if this bound is exceeded an incomplete will be awarded for the module).
  • If a student fails to hand in exactly one assignment on the relevant due date as a result of illness, the mark for his/her remaining assignment will also be awarded for that assignment, if the student is able to produce a valid medical certificate (clearly stating that the student was incapacitated for an extended period of time and hence could not work on the assignment - a medical certificate valid only for a day or two will not be acceptable). If a student fails to hand in exactly one assignment on the relevant due date as a result of any reason other than illness, an absent (zero) will be awarded for that assignment. However, if a student fails to hand in both assignments on the relevant due dates due to whatever reason, an incomplete will be awarded for the module (since the bound of 12% mentioned above will have been exceeded).
  • If a student misses a semester test as a result of illness, an oral test will be scheduled in the appropriate period for second examinations, provided that the student is able to produce a valid medical certificate. However, if a student misses a semester test due to a reason other than illness, an incomplete will be awarded for the module (since the bound of 12% mentioned above will have been exceeded).
  • Finally, a sub-minimum of 30% for each of the two semester tests is required before a student can pass the module.
  • A final mark of at least 50% is required for passing the module, provided that all of the above requirements are also met. A final mark of at least 75% is required for passing the module with distinction, provided that all of the above requirements are also met.

Daar sal elke Donderdagmiddag 'n 3-uur tutoriaal wees. Die bywoning van tutoriale is verpligtend. Tutorials


There will be a 3-hour tutorial every Thursday afternoon. Attendance of these tutorials is compulsory. During tutorials students are afforded the opportunity to discuss with the lecturer any problems they may have encountered during the module, and to work on selected exercises under the supervision of the lecturer. A short tutorial test on the work covered during the previous week may be taken down during each tutorial. These tutorial tests will count towards the student's final mark for the module, as explained under the heading "Module Assessment."

Tutorials are valuable contact sessions between the students and the lecturer and should be utilised fully, by:
  • arriving on time for tutorials,
  • preparing for tutorials beforehand (by ensuring that the theory covered during the previous week is fully understood),
  • working diligently in the computer laboratory on the exercises made available by the lecturer (and not waiting idly until the tutorial test starts), and
  • using tutorials to resolve any problems encountered with the contents of the module, because of otherwise limited time before and after lectures.

Study Hints


The following hints may prove useful when students prepare for assessment opportunities:
  1. There are markers throughout the lecture notes which indicate which exercises students should be able to attempt at various stages during the course. Students should preferably attempt as many of these exercises during the semester as possible.
  2. Students should ensure that they understand the theory underlying a specific topic covered during lectures, before they attempt exercises on that topic. Students should not attempt to understand the theory by doing exercises. Theoretical questions will form part of both semester tests as well as of tutorial tests. By getting to grips with the theory covered in this module, students can ensure for themselves between 10 and 15% of the total marks available!
  3. Students should come prepared to the weekly tutorials. Tutorials form an integral and substantial part of the final mark for this module.
  4. When preparing for semester tests, students should take the standard and nature of tutorial tests as a guage of what to expect.
  5. All work covered during lectures will be assessed during tutorial tests and semester tests.
